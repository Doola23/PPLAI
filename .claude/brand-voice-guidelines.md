# PLAI Brand Voice Guidelines
Generated: 2026-06-03 | Confidence: High

## 1. Brand Identity

**Brand:** PLAI / PLAI Analytics
**Product:** AI-powered Premier League football analytics platform
**Audience:** Premier League professionals — coaches, analysts, scouts, technical directors, sporting directors, physios, data leads, club executives. Secondary: Championship clubs, agencies.
**Positioning:** The serious analytics platform for serious football professionals. Not a consumer app. Not a stats website. A decision-making tool used inside winning clubs.

## 2. Voice Constants (Never Change)

These define PLAI's personality regardless of content type or audience.

### We Are / We Are Not

| We Are | We Are Not |
|--------|-----------|
| Confident | Arrogant |
| Precise | Pedantic |
| Insider | Exclusive |
| Urgent (quietly) | Hyped |
| Outcome-focused | Feature-focused |
| Premium | Elitist |
| Declarative | Preachy |
| Specific | Vague |
| Serious | Boring |
| Ambitious | Boastful |

### Core Personality Attributes

1. **Insider fluency** — We speak football operations. xG, pressing intensity, biomechanical scores, set-piece analysis. No definitions needed. If our reader needs a glossary, they're not our reader.
2. **Restrained confidence** — We let numbers do the talking. Superlatives come from customers, never from us.
3. **Outcome-obsessed** — Every claim anchors to a real result. Time saved, money made, injuries prevented, goals stopped.
4. **Ruthless precision** — 62,817 not "60,000+". 14 hours not "half a day". 87.2% not "nearly 90%". Specificity is our proof.
5. **Forward momentum** — Short sentences. Declarative. No hedging. "Data wins matches." Not "Data can potentially help teams perform better."

## 4. Messaging Framework

### Primary Value Propositions (in order of power)

1. **Predictive advantage** — Know things before they happen. Injury flags 3 weeks early. Match outcomes 90 minutes before kickoff.
2. **Speed** — What took 14 hours takes minutes. Scout 4,200 players in an afternoon.
3. **Accuracy** — 87.2% prediction confidence. Specific, auditable, consistent.
4. **ROI** — "He's now worth 4× what we paid." "Budget halved, insights doubled."
5. **Consolidation** — Replace 3 tools with one platform.
6. **Social proof** — The clubs at the top already use this.

### Approved Headline Formulas

- **Tension/consequence:** "Gut feel loses matches." / "Your rivals already have this."
- **Confrontational truth:** "The data doesn't miss." / "Outthink the opposition."
- **Outcome statement:** "Predict. Scout. Dominate." / "See it before kickoff."
- **Social proof challenge:** "Clubs that win, use PLAI."

## 5. Approved Terminology

### Always Use
- "analytics" (not "insights" as a standalone)
- "Premier League" in full (not "PL" in headlines)
- "predictions" / "predict" (not "forecasts" in CTAs)
- "precision" (associated with brand identity)
- "intelligence" (for platform and feature positioning)
- "flags" / "flagged" (for injury risk)
- "data-led" (not "data-driven" — overused)
- "scout engine" / "scout pipeline" (not "search tool")
- Specific numbers always (87.2% not ~87%, 62,817 not ~62,000)

### Never Use
- "seamless", "elevate", "unleash", "next-gen", "game-changer" (AI slop)
- "leverage" as a verb
- "empower" or "enable"
- Exclamation marks in brand copy
- "cutting-edge", "state-of-the-art", "revolutionary"
- Passive voice in headlines
- Round numbers when specific ones are available

### Technical Football Terms (Use Without Definition)
xG, xA, progressive carries, pressing intensity, duels won, percentile rankings, biomechanical stress scores, Monte Carlo simulations, set-piece analysis, tactical profile, matchday pack, opposition dashboard

## 6. Headline Standards

- **Length:** 2–6 words for hero headlines. Max 10 words for section headings.
- **Structure:** Declarative statements. No questions in headlines. No ellipses.
- **Fragments are intentional:** "Win More. Data First." — the period-as-pause is a brand signature.
- **No softening:** Never hedge with "can", "might", "help you". Claim it.
- **Confrontational angle preferred for hero:** Creates tension that pulls the reader in.

### Headline Quality Test
Ask: Would a top Premier League analyst read this and feel it was written for them? If it could appear on any generic SaaS product, rewrite it.

## 7. Content Examples

### Strong (On-Brand)

> "Outthink the opposition." ✓
*Short, confrontational, football-native, implies competitive advantage*

> "PLAI flagged three hamstring risks two weeks before our medical staff noticed anything." ✓
*Specific number, specific time, outcome implied, real club credibility*

> "One decision backed by data is worth more than the subscription." ✓
*ROI framing, confident, no fluff*

> "Budget halved, insights doubled." ✓
*Fragment. Specific. Outcome. Rhythm.*

### Weak (Off-Brand)

> "The analytics platform that helps football teams make better decisions." ✗
*Generic, passive benefit, no tension, no specificity*

> "Powerful AI-driven insights for your club." ✗
*"Powerful", "AI-driven", "insights" — all banned/weak terms*

> "Get started today and see the difference!" ✗
*Exclamation mark, vague outcome, generic CTA*
